# Impact-Driven Business Decisions

*Causal inference, evidence evaluation, and decision theory — connected into one operational framework. Available as a full course, a multi-day intensive, or a focused workshop.*

---

Most analytics training teaches methods in isolation — a unit on A/B testing here, a lecture on regression there. People leave with techniques but no system for connecting measurement to decisions. This is different.

**Impact-Driven Business Decisions** teaches participants to build and operate decision systems that learn. Every method — from causal inference to resource allocation — is taught as part of a single loop: measure what works, evaluate how much to trust it, decide what to do, and repeat.

## What participants walk away with

Participants who complete this material can:

- **Measure impact** using causal inference methods that distinguish correlation from causation — potential outcomes, DAGs, matching, and synthetic control
- **Evaluate evidence** systematically — assessing methodology rigor, identification assumptions, and research design quality before acting on results
- **Allocate resources** under uncertainty — using decision theory to determine where to invest based on measured impact and remaining uncertainty
- **Build systems** that make this repeatable — version control, reproducible pipelines, and software engineering practices that turn one-off analyses into durable infrastructure

## What makes this different

**One framework, not a toolbox.** The material is organized around a single principle — *Learn · Decide · Repeat* — that connects every method into an operational loop. Participants don't just learn techniques; they learn how techniques connect to decisions that generate new evidence.

**Everything runs.** Every lecture is a Jupyter notebook that executes against purpose-built open-source tools — an online retail simulator, a causal estimation engine, and an evidence review system. There is no pseudocode. Theory and working implementation sit side by side.

**One applied domain throughout.** Rather than switching contexts every week, the entire curriculum is grounded in a single business problem — improving product data quality in large-scale online retail. Participants build depth, not breadth.

**Built end-to-end by one person.** The lectures, the tools, and the infrastructure are all designed and built by Philipp Eisenhauer. The material reflects a coherent point of view on how measurement, evaluation, and decision-making connect — not a committee-assembled syllabus.

## Structure

The content follows a deliberate progression:

1. **Understand the domain** — Establish the business context and data landscape
2. **Measure impact** — Learn causal inference methods to establish what actually works
3. **Evaluate evidence** — Assess how reliable each estimate is before acting on it
4. **Allocate resources** — Use decision theory to determine where to invest
5. **Build systems** — Turn methods into reproducible, production-grade infrastructure

Each stage feeds the next. Measurement produces evidence. Evidence evaluation determines confidence. Confidence informs allocation. Allocation generates new data. The loop closes.

## Delivery formats

The modular structure adapts to different contexts and time constraints. The full curriculum runs as a 10-week university course. The core causal inference sequence works as a multi-day intensive. Individual modules — domain motivation, a single estimation method, or the evidence evaluation pipeline — stand alone as half-day or full-day sessions.

The material is designed for any setting where people need to connect measurement to decisions — graduate programs, corporate analytics teams, or executive education.
