# Evaluate Evidence

Not all measurements are equally trustworthy. Before using causal estimates to guide resource allocation, it is essential to assess the quality of the evidence itself. This section teaches how to critically evaluate causal evidence — considering methodology rigor, identification assumptions, and the strength of the research design — to determine which estimates are reliable enough to act on.
